Research

Teaching machines to read
the language of disease

Our lab develops deep learning approaches that integrate medical imaging, pathology, and genomic data to drive precision medicine — working at the intersection of radiology, oncology, cardiology, and bioinformatics.

⬡
Radiomics & deep radiomics
Quantifying thousands of imaging features from CT, PET, and MRI to build non-invasive biomarkers predicting tumour biology and survival. Our seminal 2014 Nature Comm. paper has 4,600+ citations.
◈
Foundation models for medicine
Large-scale self-supervised models pre-trained on 11 million medical images to discover novel cancer imaging biomarkers — dramatically reducing reliance on labelled training data.
♡
AI in cardiology — DeepCAC
Predicting cardiovascular risk from routine chest X-rays and CT. Linking cancer therapy toxicity to heart disease via AI-automated body composition. Featured on CNN Morning Show, 2022.
◉
FaceAge — biological ageing
Deep learning system estimating biological age from facial photographs, achieving ~80% accuracy predicting cancer patient survival. Published in Lancet Digital Health, 2025. Covered by NYT, WaPo, CNN, BBC.
⌬
Radiogenomics
Linking imaging phenotypes to molecular and genomic profiles — developing multimodal AI that integrates CT scans, genomics, and pathology slides to understand tumour biology non-invasively.
⬚
LLMs in clinical care
Evaluating and developing large language models for patient messaging, cancer treatment recommendations, and extraction of social determinants of health from clinical notes.
